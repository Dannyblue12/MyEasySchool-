15 Scholarships for Nigerian Students with Low CGPA (Below 4.0) – Apply Now

Looking for scholarships in Nigeria but your CGPA is below 4.0? Don't worry! Here are 15 real scholarships for Nigerian students with low CGPA, plus how to apply.

MyEasySchool Team

Educational Content Writer
January 15, 2025
12 min read

Are you a student worried that your low CGPA means you can't win a scholarship? Take a deep breath, you're not alone. Many students believe scholarships are only for first-class or straight-A students. But the truth is, several scholarship opportunities exist for students with average or even low GPAs, and I'm going to guide you through them step by step.

This post is not just a list, it's a practical guide to help you apply successfully. Whether your CGPA is 2.5 or 3.0, you still stand a chance. Let's dive in!

15 Scholarships for Nigerian Students with Low CGPA

Here are some of the most reliable scholarships you can apply for in 2025:

1. NNPC/SNEPCo National Scholarship

Eligibility: Nigerian undergraduate students in accredited universities.

Minimum CGPA: 2.5/5.0 (Second Class Lower).

Steps to Apply:

1. Visit the official application portal: www.spdc-jv.com.

2. Fill in your personal and academic details correctly.

3. Upload scanned copies of your admission letter, school ID card, and recent passport photograph.

4. Submit and wait for a confirmation email.

Deadline: Usually between April and June.

2. Agbami Medical and Engineering Scholarship

Eligibility: Students studying Medicine, Engineering, or related courses.

Minimum CGPA: 2.5/5.0.

How to Apply:

1. Go to www.chevron.com/agbami.

2. Register with your email and create a profile.

3. Upload required documents: School ID card, Admission letter, WAEC/NECO result.

4. Submit and keep your reference number.

3. Jim Ovia Foundation Scholarship

Eligibility: Open to all Nigerian undergraduates, regardless of CGPA.

How to Apply:

1. Visit www.jimoviafoundation.org.

2. Create an account and fill in your details.

3. Upload academic transcripts, admission letter, and ID card.

4. Write a short essay about your academic goals.

Tip: Be honest and passionate in your essay—it matters!

4. David Oyedepo Foundation Scholarship

Eligibility: Students in Covenant and Landmark Universities, but international students can also apply.

How to Apply:

1. Go to www.davidoyedepofoundation.org.

2. Fill the application form and upload necessary documents.

3. Write an essay on your vision and how this scholarship will help.

5. Federal Government Scholarship (FSBN)

Eligibility: Undergraduate and postgraduate students in Nigerian universities.

Steps:

1. Register on fsbn.com.ng.

2. Fill the form accurately.

3. Print the exam slip and prepare for the CBT exam.

6. Tony Elumelu Foundation (TEF) Entrepreneurship Program

This is for students with business ideas.

Steps:

1. Visit tefconnect.net.

2. Create a profile and submit your business idea.

3. If selected, you'll receive training and $5,000 seed funding.

7. Mobil Undergraduate Scholarship

Eligibility: Engineering and Geoscience students.

Steps:

1. Apply via mobilproducingnigeria.com.

2. Fill in your personal info and upload your documents.

8. Petroleum Technology Development Fund (PTDF)

Eligibility: For those interested in oil and gas-related fields.

Steps:

1. Visit ptdf.gov.ng.

2. Create an account, fill your details, and upload documents.

3. Submit and prepare for selection interviews.

9. Seplat Scholarship

Eligibility: Second-year students and above in Nigerian universities.

Steps:

1. Apply via seplatpetroleum.com.

2. Upload required documents and submit your application.

10. Addax Petroleum Scholarship

Eligibility: For undergraduates in specific fields.

Steps:

1. Go to addaxpetroleum.com.

2. Complete the form and upload your documents.

11. NNPC/Total Scholarship

Steps:

1. Visit scholarships.totalenergies.com.

2. Create an account, fill the application, and upload documents.

12. NLNG Scholarship

Eligibility: First-year undergraduates.

Steps:

1. Apply via nlng.com.

2. Submit documents like admission letter, WAEC result, and passport photo.

13. NWAG (Nigerian Women Association of Georgia) Scholarship

Eligibility: Female undergraduates in Nigeria.

Steps:

1. Download the application form from nwag.org.

2. Write an essay and submit with required documents.

14. Guinness Nigeria Scholarship

Eligibility: Engineering and science students.

Steps:

1. Apply via guinness-nigeria.com.

2. Upload your credentials and submit the form.

15. MTN Foundation Scholarship

Eligibility: Nigerian students in any field of study.

Steps:

1. Visit mtnonline.com/foundation.

2. Complete the online application form.

3. Upload required documents and submit.

✅ Pro Tips to Increase Your Chances

Start early—don't wait for deadlines. Prepare scanned copies of your documents. Write strong essays (be authentic and passionate). Apply for multiple scholarships to increase your chances.

✅ FAQs

Q1: Can I apply for more than one scholarship?

Yes, you can apply for as many as you qualify for.

Q2: Do I need to be first-class to win these scholarships?

No! Many accept 2.5 CGPA or don't require CGPA at all.

Q3: Are these scholarships legit?

Yes, all listed here are genuine and well-known.

Final Thoughts

Your CGPA does not define your future, your determination does. Scholarships are not just for the smartest students, they're for students who take action. So, start applying today and secure the financial help you deserve.
